How do the products you buy affect your community?

Products you buy will strongly affect the community you live in, because if you buy local products, then you will keep the money in your community because buying a service, or a product will go to the owner, who will buy, something else locally, until it becomes taken away by the municipal government, as tax. In another scenario, you buy something that was not local, and this will make the money scatter around the globe, resulting in your community economy not growing.



How do your consumer choices affect someone across the world?

The products that you chose to buy as a consumer can affect other consumer choices by increasing the value of a company and therefore making the company more popular, and increasing sales more. Next, most sales are done online, mostly as a result of COVID-19, so many customers are concerned about their data so scammers in foreign countries have lower chances of committing wire fraud. Lastly, buying something from a form a foreign country might be risky because you don't know who the money is being sent to, so this might be used to violate human rights.



What does it mean to violate human rights?

Human rights are generally the idea that we humans as a people are all born with an entitlement to certain things just from our birth. The idea is that as human beings we can be respected with a life of equal dignity and general respect. Human rights show us what we should not do and it helps us treat each other in a manner that lets us grow together as a species rather than thousands of individual groups. To violate human rights is the robbery of that respect that is granted to all humans, and to violate them is to mean disunity and discord in our race, which is the exact opposite of what the United Nations aims for in society.



What were the banana republics?

“Banana Republics” is defined in political science as a politically unstable country with an economy dependent upon the export of natural resources, however, we need to dive deeper if we truly want to understand what a “Banana Republic” is. The term Banana Republic can be traced back to 1904 when author O. Henry Coined described Honduras and neighbouring countries in Central America as “Banana Republics”. These countries were titled this name for their large economic exploitation by the US government in the category of banana cultivation, these countries went on to almost become completely dependent on bananas and the only outcome of this situation was more cash put into the pockets of the US elite.



What is fair trade and what are the benefits of buying fair trade products?

Fair trade is a system of certification that aims to ensure a set of standards in the production of goods and supply to give worker’s the basic set of rights they deserve. This means safe working conditions, fair pay and the right to disassociate themselves from furthermore production of the product by their hands if they actively wish to halt their work. For us, it means higher quality products which are created ethically. Fairtrade advocates for a thriving environment for workers and the thought for the customer that they are not supporting an unjust company that abuses their workers and puts them at risk of danger or abuse.



How to avoid purchasing products that did not originate from fair trade?

Purchasing products that originate from fair trade can reward many benefits for yourself and the world in general. Fairtrade can fight poverty, inequality and other forms of injustice all while delivering you, the customer an all-around better product than an item that was created with cheap or unfair labour. Fairtrade seeks greater equity in international relations and looks for better trade deals for all nations alike. Stacked on top of all of this, fair trade is necessary if we want to achieve our 17 sustainable development goals by the year 2030. Fairtrade is important for you, me and everyone we know. Support fair trade by buying products that were created using it.
